Industry Overview

The global esports market size was valued at USD 1.1 billion in 2019 and is expected to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 24.4% from 2020 to 2027. The increasing audience reach and engagement activities, formidable investments, rising live streaming of games, and increasing infrastructure for the league tournaments are key factors driving the market growth. The professionalization in the industry has created lucrative opportunities for game developers, gamers, influencers, and event organizers. Millennials are considering esports as a professional career owing to the increase in popularity of the gaming tournaments, impressive international prize pools, streaming revenues, and one-to-one sponsorships. Moreover, universities and colleges are starting a dedicated esports curriculum to develop skilled professionals.

Source: Grand View Research

Revenue Source Insights

The sponsorship segment dominated the global esports revenue, with a market share of 39.9% in 2019. Sponsorship offers a sizeable opportunity for various brands to directly reach to the target audience through offline and online media channels. Booths, posters, interactive advertising, video displays, freebies, and many other creative methods facilitate the brand to target millions of fans. Many endemic brands have already well-established sponsorship deals with esports teams and leagues.

Additionally, the industry has seen an up stick from non-endemic brands such as Volkswagen Group, Daimler AG (Mercedes Benz), McDonald's Corporation, Tinder, and Vivo Communication Technology Co. Ltd. as a key sponsor for the esports tournaments and teams. There have been nearly 76 deals by non-endemic brands for Q1 2019, which includes prominent brands such as The Coca-Cola Company; AT&T Inc.; Nissan Motor Co., Ltd.; and Nike, Inc.

The media rights segment is one of the substantial revenue-generating streams and is estimated to expand at the highest CAGR of over 25.5% over the forecast period. Media rights include all the revenues paid to event organizers, teams, and leagues in order to secure the rights to broadcast esports content on a channel. esports consists of more than thousands of individual leagues, events, and championships for which the media rights deal is expected to generate a substantial amount of revenue in the coming years. Twitch is one of the prominent streaming platforms used by fans to watch major tournaments.

Source: Grand View Research

Timeline: The Rise of Esports

1972

Earliest video game competition, the Intergalactic Spacewar Olympics, takes place at Stanford University for the game Spacewar.

1980

Atari holds the Space Invaders Championship, featuring over 10,000 players.

1983

Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) takes controls, graphics, gameplay, and accessibility of video games to a new level.

1989

Sega Genesis is released.

1990

Nintendo World Championships tours the U.S., holding championship games in California.

1996~1997

The first eSports leagues are founded: Quakecon, Cyberathlete Professional League (CPL), and Professional Gamers League.

1997

Red Annihilation, a Quake competitive events, is launched. This is widely considered one of the first true eSports competitions.

2006

FUN Technologies holds a Worldwide Webgames Championship, hosting 71 players and a grand prize of $1 million.

2010

Nintendo returns to the scene with the Wii Games Championship: a tournament that lasts over a month, with over 400,000 players.

2011

Twitch gives eSports a platform to reach previously unthinkable heights. Games like League of Legends (LoL) and Defense of the Ancients 2 (Dota 2) have received millions of views.

2011

First 'International' Dota 2 tournament (grand prize of $1 million) and LoL World Championship (pirze pool of $100,000) are held.

2014

Amazon buys Twitch for $970 million.

2016

LoL World Championships brings in a staggering 43 million viewers. At its peak, there were 14.7 million simultaneous viewers.

2019

Google enters the market with the announcement of its new streaming service, Stadia.
